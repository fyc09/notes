# English

## Listen & Speaking

### Well-organized Notes

- parts: Topic, Big picture, Key points, Important details
- Symbols Abbreviations

### Summary (Detailed)

- S1: Identification (Source, Speaker, Topic, Main idea)
  - e.g. In the lecture about …, the lecturer states that … by …
- S2~: Big picture, key points, important details
- Remark. Use formal words.

### Types of Communication

1. Informative communication: using information question.
   - General information questions
     - e.g. What is the interview about?
   - Specific information questions
     - e.g. What is the origin name of …?
2. Critical communication: using critical questions.
   - Remembering, Understanding, Applying, Analyzing, Evaluating, Creating.

Remark.

- Types of Argumentations:
  1. Opinion: To express the Author's opinion.
  2. Persuasion: To persuade the reader.
  3. Argumentative: To argue some opinion is incorrect.
- Modes of Persuasion: Ethos, Pathos, Logos.
- Elements of Narration: Time, Place, Characters, Events, Order, Point of View.
- Plot: Exposition, Rising Action, Climax, Falling Action, Resolution.
- Organizational Patterns:
  - Spatial order
  - Chronological order
  - Impressionistic order
  - Topic pattern
  - Extended definition
  - Classification / Division
  - Process (directional or informational)
  - Comparison &/or Contrast
  - Cause & Effect
    - Focus on Causes
    - Focus on Effects
  - Problem–Solution
  - Pros & Cons
- Rhetorical Devices:
  - personification
  - analogy
  - comparison
  - metaphor
  - simile
  - exaggeration
  - parallelism
  - repetition
  - _situational / verbal_ irony
  - contrast
  - alliteration
  - imagery

### Rhetorical Précis

4 Sentences:

1. Main Idea
   - Author
   - Title
   - Type of text
   - Topic or main idea
   - Use a reporting verb
2. Development
   - Supporting ideas
   - How the ideas are developed
   - Organization or structure
   - Methods used (definition, examples, comparison, etc.)
3. Purpose
   - Author's purpose
   - Use “in order to”
   - What the author wants readers to do, think, or understand?
4. Audience & Language
   - Target audience
   - Language style
   - Tone or level of formality
   - Rhetorical effectiveness
